Shimla: The Queen of Hills

Perched at an altitude of 2,200 meters, Shimla is a charming hill station that once served as the summer capital of British India. Known for its colonial architecture, bustling bazaars, and scenic landscapes, Shimla offers a perfect blend of history and natural beauty.

Dharamshala: The Little Lhasa of India

Nestled in the Kangra Valley, Dharamshala is a serene town that serves as the spiritual and cultural hub of Tibetan Buddhism in India. The town is divided into two parts: Lower Dharamshala and McLeod Ganj, home to the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile.

Leh-Ladakh: The Land of High Passes

A dream destination for adventure lovers, Leh-Ladakh is a high-altitude desert known for its rugged terrain, turquoise lakes, and Buddhist monasteries. It's a paradise for bikers, trekkers, and nature enthusiasts.
